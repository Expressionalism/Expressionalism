# **Expressionalism Glossary**

This glossary supplies precise, rigorous, and accessible definitions for every major term, concept, toggle, metric, and mathematical construct in the Expressionalism ecosystem. It draws directly from the Framework (Presumptions 1–8), the Toolkit operational procedures, the Mathematical Model, and the Metric Validation Results. Every entry remains explicitly provisional in keeping with P8’s open perception gate.

The system treats the creation, use, or revision of this glossary itself as a new primary expression subject to the same P1–P8 analysis. The Open Field stays open.

## **How to Use This Glossary**

Consult the Terminology block for concepts, toggles, and explanations. Consult the Mathematics and Equations block for formal definitions, formulas, and validated behaviors. Cross-references appear in **bold** for quick navigation.

## **A**

### **Adaptive Depth**

Toolkit toggle that automatically chooses Lite mode (P1–P6 only) for simpler inputs or Full depth for richer ones. It includes an Obtainment Focus sub-mode that emphasizes contrasts and obtainability in P1–P2. In practice, it reduces unnecessary complexity for routine work while keeping full analytical power available when needed. The toggle activates based on input variance and ambiguity score.

### **Alien Mode**

Toolkit toggle that forces the system to work with raw bytes, bits, or pixels for every input. It turns off standard assumptions such as the **Perspective lens**, widens sigma by \+0.15, and raises uncertainty when resonance falls below 0.45. This mode helps prevent relational assumptions from creeping into raw sensory data, abstract art, or pre-linguistic phenomena. It works especially well with **Phenomenal Probe**.

### **Alignability Spectrum (P6)**

The range of how easily secondary expressions can be compared with the primary expression. It runs from sparse (minimal connections and non-relational gaps) through partial (mid-level translated phenomena) to dense (strong native alignments). This spectrum supplies the distances and differences used for measurement under P6.

### **Alignment**

The degree to which a secondary expression supports and coheres with the current primary expression. Higher alignment strengthens the **Secondary Coherence Score** and indicates more reliable secondary synthesis.

### **Ambiguity Score**

A value calculated in Stage 1 that measures how coherent the input chunks are. High scores automatically trigger Light Output and widen sigma for more porous material.

### **Atomic Expression (P4)**

A layered expression made of a sequence of relateds that functions as a relator. It can stand alone as a related when measured by itself but operates relationally inside larger structures. See also **Layer Spectrum (P4)**, **Related**, and **Relator**.

## **B**

### **Binomial Falsification Threshold**

A statistical check (p \= 0.18 with \+5% reflexive tolerance) that flags persistent incoherence. It uses data-type thresholds of 25% for expressions and 30% for phenomena across 15 perturbations. When the threshold is exceeded, the system produces partial yields with boosted uncertainties. Validation shows 0% false positives below 20% incidence and 100% correct triggering above the threshold.

## **C**

### **Certainty Temper Index**

A Phase 5 metric (typically 0.3–0.7) calculated as 1 minus layer spectrum times (1 minus 0.15 times uncertainty probability global). It shows how much reflexive doubt has softened the original certainties. Lower values indicate stronger tempering from Phase 5\.

### **Certainty Temper Probe**

Toolkit toggle that runs Deep reflexive certainty tempering in Phase 5\. It includes Light and Deep sub-modes and works together with **Uncertainty Probe**.

### **Chaos Scale**

A tunable parameter (default 0.05) that increases porosity and fairness for chaotic or uncertain inputs. Higher values widen sigma and raise the uncertainty upper bound.

### **Complex Expression (P4)**

A layered expression made of nests that contain multiple relateds and at least one relator. It functions as a standalone related while allowing unlimited nesting depth. See also **Layer Spectrum (P4)**.

### **Connectability Gradient (P3)**

One of the three co-emergent dimensions of relational density. It measures how fully a contrasting part can participate in relational construction versus gesturing toward unconnectable gaps. See also **Relational Density Spectrum (P3)** and **Non-Relational Remainder / Gaps**.

### **Contrarian**

Toolkit toggle that lowers input variance and actively probes non-relational gaps. It often activates automatically on high ambiguity scores.

### **Contrasting Parts (P2)**

The variances, fluid degrees, or uncertainties that appear once existence obtains under P1’s Door 1\. They create the differentiations needed for later relational construction. See also **The Open Field (P1)** and **Relational Construction (P3)**.

### **Current Primary Expression**

The immediate totality being analyzed. During analysis it is treated primarily as a related at roughly 1 certainty. It co-exists with secondary expressions under P5. See also **Primary Expression** and **Related**.

## **D**

### **Data Type**

The automatic classification of an input as either an expression or a phenomenon. This classification adjusts incoherence thresholds and sigma. Phenomena receive wider sigma and higher uncertainty boosts.

### **Decomposition Gate (P3)**

The operation that breaks repeatable relational paths into relateds (passive anchors) and relators (active connectors) operating in one of three provisional modes. See also **Related**, **Relator**, and **Relational Density Spectrum (P3)**.

### **Density Probe**

Toolkit toggle that computes internal variances and layering metrics in Phases 1 and 2\. It includes Base, Fuzzy, Weighted, and Iterative sub-modes.

### **Dilemma Gate (P1)**

The mechanism that evaluates obtainability through the hybrid function H(x). If the result exceeds τ \= 0.001, analysis proceeds through Door 1 with relational material available. If it falls at or below τ \= 0.001, the system registers generative uncertainty or halts at Door 2, with reciprocal boosting of downstream uncertainty metrics. See also **The Open Field (P1)** and **Hybrid Function H(x)**.

### **Directional Bleed**

A sub-toggle under **Secondary Expression Equity** that enables bidirectional resonance analysis. Asymmetry above 0.2 increases tension and fragility measures.

### **Dirichlet Priors**

The equity-balancing sampling method used in Phase 2\. It incorporates void-prior alphas (0.37 for high input variance) so that non-relational gaps receive fair representation.

## **E**

### **Echo Mode**

A sub-toggle under **Uncertainty Probe** that applies tradition-decay via priors for multiple uncertainties. It supports emptiness-focused analyses.

### **Emptiness-First**

Toolkit toggle that reverses the usual relational bias. Non-relational gaps become the primary lens while relational utilities become provisional. It raises the uncertainty upper bound and treats high-uncertainty yields as valuable rather than deficient.

### **Equity Sampler**

A sub-toggle under **Secondary Expression Equity** that perturbs noise from Dirichlet priors. It increases uncertainty yield for marginal or underrepresented cases.

## **F**

### **Falsify Mode**

Toolkit toggle that tightens or loosens incoherence thresholds (Lenient adds \+5% tolerance; Strict subtracts 5%).

### **Feedback-Adaptive**

A sub-toggle under **Uncertainty Probe** that retro-tempers Phase 1 and 2 metrics after Phases 2 and 3\. Shifts greater than 0.1 trigger “Recursive Boost” notes for P8 consideration.

### **Finite Multiplicity (P5)**

The middle range of the MultiSpectrum. It represents bounded clusters of secondary expressions and serves as the default pragmatic yield. See also **Plurality of Expressions (P5)**.

### **Fragility Adjustment (Harmony Index)**

A mechanism that activates exactly when metric variance exceeds 0.12 and raises the Harmony Index by approximately 0.12–0.15. It rewards productive tension rather than penalizing it. Validation confirms it activates at the designed threshold with consistent uplift.

### **Functional Role Shift**

The perceptual and interpretive process at the heart of P8. It allows any node to move between passive (related) and active (relator) status or to change relational mode during re-expression. Roles are recorded as metadata outside the core numerical model and remain context-dependent.

## **G**

### **Harmony Index**

A Phase 6 metric computed as the harmonic mean of yields plus a fragility adjustment when variance exceeds 0.12. It measures balanced interplay within tension.

## **I**

### **Incoherence Flag**

An explicit marker triggered when incidence exceeds data-type-aware thresholds across 15 perturbations with standard deviation greater than 0.30. It produces partial yields with boosted uncertainties.

### **Infinite Multiplicity (P5)**

The high end of the MultiSpectrum. It represents boundless proliferations of secondary expressions and activates only where P2 contrasts suggest unbounded variance. See also **Plurality of Expressions (P5)**.

### **Inferred Relator Mode Distribution**

A derived probabilistic distribution over the three provisional relational modes (Self-reflexive, Other relational, Non-rel-probe). It is computed exclusively from finalized Phase 5–6 metrics using log-odds formulation followed by softmax normalization.

The distribution is presented in grouped form with conditional probabilities and always carries the required labeling that the probabilities are interpretive only and that functional roles remain context-dependent under P8.

Validation shows the model is directionally correct: high Synthesis Refusal proportion strongly increases P(Non-rel-probe); high reflexive tension and doubt increase P(Self-reflexive); all probabilities sum exactly to 1.0; and the maximum probability shift under ±20% coefficient perturbation is 0.0565.

### **Input Variance**

The aggregated standard deviation of fuzzy memberships from P1–P4 spectra across chunks. It is clamped between 0.03 and 0.6 and directly influences sigma and uncertainty probability global.

### **Inverse Mode**

A sub-toggle under **Uncertainty Probe** that back-feeds phantom certainties when stability falls below 0.5. It turns voids provisional for later P8 renewal.

## **L**

### **Layer Spectrum (P4)**

A hierarchical spectrum ranging from low (subatomic lone relateds) through mid (atomic sequences functioning as relators) to high (complex nests). It inherits from P3 relational density. See also **Atomic Expression (P4)**, **Complex Expression (P4)**, and **Subatomic Expression (P4)**.

### **Layer Spectrum Avg**

A supporting diagnostic (Phase 1\) that averages layered decompositions across the range 0.2–0.8 when Density Probe is active.

### **Light Output**

Toolkit toggle that collapses all tables and ledgers into a single flowing narrative paragraph anchored on the **Secondary Coherence Score** while preserving every yield and key tension.

### **Light Output Narrative**

The concise prose version of any toolkit run produced when Light Output is active. It still references the **Secondary Coherence Score** as the central measure.

## **M**

### **Meaning Tension**

The ratio of certainty geometric average divided by (certainty geometric average plus uncertainty yield geometric average). It typically ranges from 0.2 to 0.5 and rises when uncertainty falls. It works together with **Reflexive Tension** to show the living balance between what holds and what remains uncertain.

### **Meta-Loop**

Toolkit toggle that automatically re-runs the yield as a new primary expression (up to three iterations). Sub-modes include Rerun-Secondaries, Rephrase-Uncertainties, Max-Uncertainties, Max-Certainties, Contrast-Runs, and Doubt-Rerun.

### **Metric Evolution Table**

Stage 3 output showing how all major metrics change across Phases 1–6.

## **N**

### **Non-Dual**

Toolkit toggle that boosts mid-paradoxes and raises tolerance above 0.9. It often activates automatically on keywords such as “void” or “paradox.”

### **Non-Relational Remainder / Gaps (P3)**

That which necessarily exceeds and resists every relational construction, including the construction of its own negation. It functions as the ever-present limit and horizon. Non-relational gaps register only as remainders of expressions where secondary synthesis fails or is refused. They are never treated as symmetric positive entities inside the schema. Protection occurs operationally through weak non-rel-probe mode and explicit “Synthesis Refusal active — no pointing back” labeling. See also **Non-Rel-Probe Mode (P3)** and **Synthesis Refusal**.

### **Non-Rel-Probe Mode (P3)**

One of the three provisional relational modes. It performs a minimal relational gesture toward what may be an unconnectable gap (or toward nothing at all) while deliberately increasing downstream uncertainty. The mode is kept deliberately weak to enact operational protection of the non-relational remainder. See also **Inferred Relator Mode Distribution** and **Non-Relational Remainder / Gaps (P3)**.

A Phase 6 metric computed as Harmony tempered by (1 plus 0.2 divided by (1 plus uncertainty probability global average)). It quantifies overall relational utility.
