## **Overview**

The Expressionalism Toolkit actively applies the eight presumptions of the framework to any primary expression. It treats pointing as the directed, representational, or reflexive aspect of an expression. A primary expression may be a linguistic statement, scientific claim, work of art, personal experience, memory, neuron firing, bird call, song, movie, political document, logical paradox, visual scene, pattern of marks in sand, image never previously encountered, or moment of silence.

The toolkit processes every input as an undifferentiated datum through the same ontologically permissive workflow. It derives connectable certainties from provisional secondary expressions and explicitly identifies locations where secondary synthesis fails or is refused. Meaning is measured as the tensive ratio between those certainties and the uncertainties that remain. Secondary expressions form the actual engine of the system: the toolkit constructs every primary expression from prior secondary expressions whose coherent meshing and pointing-back determine its standing. Where secondary synthesis is absent or refused, uncertainty arises because there is no pointing back — this is Synthesis Refusal, the measurable limit of any secondary-synthesis audit.

P1 serves as the ontologically open permissive synthesis field within which the toolkit abducts and aligns secondary expressions prior to reverse-engineering the secondary synthesis that constitutes the current primary expression and examining its pointing-aspect at P8. The toolkit accepts linguistic, phenomenal, visual, multimodal, or any other datum, maintaining full openness across domains while treating secondary expressions as static, reliable anchors for the duration of each run. All outputs remain provisional, fallible, and subject to the framework’s own grammar.

## **Core Rules at a Glance**

* Secondary Coherence Score is the primary diagnostic metric in standard runs (computed exclusively on non-isolated components).  
* Hard Isolation (traction ≤ τ \= 0.001) is non-negotiable: isolated components are excluded from all relational metrics, scores, and distributions.  
* Inferred Relator Mode Distribution recognizes only two relational modes (Self-reflexive, Pointing to something else) plus Residual non-relational complement.  
* Everything is provisional and subject to re-expression under P8.  
* Meaning emerges as the productive tension between connectable certainties and the uncertainties, generative pauses, and non-relational gaps that necessarily remain.

Non-rel-probe functions as a weak flag that triggers hard isolation rather than as a third relational mode).

Relateds and relators are functional roles revealed in analysis, not intrinsic properties. The toolkit treats the input primary expression primarily as a related whose pointing-aspect is being assessed; secondary expressions function as relators. Functional Role Shift is central to P8. The toolkit computes the Inferred Relator Mode Distribution exclusively on non-isolated components and displays it with Residual (non-relational complement).

If layered expressions obtain, they manifest as a current primary expression (treated during analysis primarily as a related at \~1 certainty) co-existing with provisional others (secondary expressions as transduced multiples). **Purpose**

This stage assumes acceptance of P1–P3 unless deep triage is explicitly selected. It customizes for domain and equity while avoiding early halts for most inputs. **Input Scan**

The toolkit transduces the input into input variance. Text receives semantic-density-normalized variance via abductive partitioning into logical chunks using thematic coherence. Images yield pixel gradients and clusters. PDFs supply keyword density from snippets. URLs undergo simulated fetch and processing. Phenomenal or non-linguistic inputs trigger auto-detection of data\_type \= ‘phenomenon’ with sigma widened to 0.12 for porous flows. Explicit transduction rules convert phenomena into descriptor chunks. Low input variance produces an early uncertainty\_prob\_global boost (+0.15 for sparse sensations). Proto-presumptions are abducted from chunks. All secondary expressions — linguistic, phenomenal, or transduced — are held static for the current analysis.

Within P1’s ontologically open field, the toolkit performs Secondary Abduction by drawing candidate secondary expressions using Dirichlet priors for equity-balanced sampling across domains and traditions.
