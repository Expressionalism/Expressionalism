# **Expressionalism: Acknowledgements to Thinkers, Traditions, and Formal Resources**

This document acknowledges the philosophical traditions, concepts, and formal resources that supplied generative starting points for Expressionalism. The framework is explicitly a synthesis. Its four distinct contributions are:

* The absolute ontological neutrality established by Presumption 1’s total-field dilemma gate, which neither privileges nor excludes any substantive metaphysical commitment while advancing its own minimal relational ontology.  
* The strict, non-redundant dialectical inheritance chain across exactly eight presumptions, in which each presumption emerges directly from the preceding ones without circularity or external imposition.  
* The explicit non-relational ring-fence, operationalized in the toolkit as measurable Synthesis Refusal with explicit “traction \= 0” and “secondary synthesis absent or refused — no pointing back” labeling, enforced through weak non-rel-probe mode and default interpretive restraint.  
* The companion toolkit and unified mathematical model that render the entire schema auditable through continuous spectra, Phase 5 reflexive certainty tempering, Secondary Presumption Temper (SPT) scoring, the Secondary Coherence Score as primary diagnostic, the Inferred Relator Mode Distribution derived via log-odds and softmax normalization, the hybrid function H(x), dilemma gates with threshold τ \= 0.001, and Light Output narratives.

Every notion and thinker below is treated as a provisional secondary expression. None functions as a foundational authority, exhaustive truth, or final doctrine. Expressionalism remains ontologically neutral and fallible: it draws selectively from these sources only insofar as they illuminate the P1–P8 presumptions, the toolkit mechanics, or the mathematical model, then subjects them to the same certainty–uncertainty tensions that every expression undergoes. The framework stands or falls on its own internal coherence, empirical behavior in live runs, and provisional utility.

## **P1 – The Open Field: Ontological Neutrality, Dilemma Gate, and Non-Relational Halts**

The P1 total field functions as an ontologically permissive synthesis space that neither privileges nor excludes any substantive metaphysical commitment while advancing its own minimal relational ontology. It opens inquiry through a dilemma gate (Door 1 proceeds with relational sparks available for further distinctions; Door 2 registers generative uncertainty or halts to silence with reciprocal uncertainty boosts) and protects non-relational remainders from colonization through operational mechanisms rather than ontological claims.

**Gorgias of Leontini (c. 483–375 BCE)** advances in On Non-Existence the tripartite claim that nothing exists; if anything exists, it cannot be known; and if it can be known, it cannot be communicated. This radical sophistic performance serves as an ideal test case for P1’s Door 2, ensuring that non-relational possibilities receive equitable space and that the framework never collapses into dogmatic affirmation of obtainability.

**Heraclitus of Ephesus (c. 535–475 BCE)** declared that one cannot step twice into the same river and that reality is governed by an underlying logos amid constant flux. He supplies a dynamic model for the total field as both ceaselessly changing and coherently ordered, informing P1’s capacity to host both relational construction and generative pauses without privileging stasis or fixed essence.

**Laozi (Lao Tzu)** presents the Dao in the Dao De Jing as the ineffable, effortless source that flows through and harmonizes all things without coercion or fixed essence. This non-forced, non-hierarchical openness offers a classical Eastern parallel to P1’s ontologically permissive starting point and its refusal of any built-in teleology or drive toward pointing.

**Nāgārjuna (c. 150–250 CE)**, founder of the Madhyamaka school and author of the Mūlamadhyamakakārikā, articulates the doctrine of śūnyatā (emptiness). All phenomena lack independent essence (svabhāva) and arise only through dependent origination (pratītyasamutpāda). Madhyamaka negation proceeds without positing any final ground or ultimate reality. This provides one of the clearest ancient articulations of the non-relational ring-fence and the reciprocal uncertainty boosts that prevent relational creep while preserving generative openness—directly paralleling P1’s protection of what necessarily exceeds every relational construction.

**Parmenides of Elea (c. 515–450 BCE)** affirms in his poem On Nature that “it is” and that being is unchanging, complete, and one. This supplies the crisp affirmative pole for P1’s dilemma gate. The framework retains the logical force of this affirmation while tempering it with Heraclitean flux and Nāgārjunian emptiness, ensuring the total field remains genuinely open rather than closed under any single ontology.

## **P2 – Contrasting Parts in the Field**

P2 registers that if existence obtains under P1’s Door 1, it must form contrasting parts or degrees of variance; otherwise the field regresses to undifferentiated uniformity that stalls meaning.

**Henri Bergson (1859–1941)** develops the concepts of durée (duration) as continuous, qualitative becoming and élan vital as creative impetus toward novelty. These model contrasts that remain fluid and processual rather than static binaries, informing P2’s deviation spectrum and the necessity of dynamic variances for any expressible field.

**Gilles Deleuze (1925–1995) and Félix Guattari (1930–1992)** describe assemblages and rhizomes as centerless, proliferating networks of connections without hierarchical origin or terminus. These provide a powerful image of P2’s contrasting parts as open, non-hierarchical webs capable of generating multiplicity without collapse into uniformity.

**Alfred North Whitehead (1861–1947)** articulates in Process and Reality an ontology in which actual occasions prehend one another into novel unities. This supplies a relational, non-substantial model for the active, processual contrasts that constitute the field. Prehensions function as the basic “feelings” or grasps through which contrasts become usable for further construction.

**Gregory Bateson (1904–1980)** developed the cybernetic insight that information is “a difference which makes a difference.” This formulation powerfully parallels P2’s contrasting parts and the framework’s treatment of pointing as the selective registration of relational difference. Bateson’s emphasis on context, pattern, and the relational nature of information also resonates with the toolkit’s use of resonance, stability, and alignment metrics.

## **P3 – Relational Construction of Contrasting Entities**

P3 constitutes the conceptual heart of the framework. Contrasting parts undergo relational construction: they bifurcate into connectable relational paths or unconnectable non-relational gaps; repeatability supplies the minimal persistence required for any pointing; and decomposition yields passive relateds and active relators operating in self-mode, other-mode, or the deliberately weak non-rel-probe mode. The non-relational remainder is ring-fenced and registers only as the measurable limit of secondary synthesis (Synthesis Refusal).

**Early Buddhist Dependent Origination and Nāgārjuna’s Madhyamaka** establish that all phenomena arise interdependently and lack independent essence. This classical articulation of conditioned arising directly parallels P3’s relational construction, while the Madhyamaka commitment to emptiness without final ground supplies the strict non-relational ring-fence that prevents relational creep.

**Charles Sanders Peirce (1839–1914)** develops a triadic semiotics of sign (representamen), object, and interpretant that constitutes the single most precise historical parallel to P3’s decomposition into relateds and relators. The three provisional relational modes (self-reflexive, other-directed, and non-rel-probe) extend Peirce’s insight into a spectrum of pointing that deliberately includes a minimal, uncertain gesture toward what cannot be fully captured. Peirce’s pragmatic maxim and thoroughgoing fallibilism underwrite the toolkit’s provisional yields, uncertainty clamping, Phase 5 reflexive tempering, and the Inferred Relator Mode Distribution. His influence is foundational and pervasive across the entire system.

**Ferdinand de Saussure (1857–1913)** articulates a differential system of signs in which meaning arises from oppositions rather than positive content. This supports P3’s bifurcation into connectable relational paths versus unconnectable gaps and reinforces the framework’s refusal to treat any element as self-sufficient.

**Ludwig Wittgenstein (1889–1951)** insists in the Tractatus that “what cannot be spoken of must be passed over in silence” and later recognizes the limits of language games. These insights reinforce P3’s non-rel-probe mode and the deliberate operational protection of non-relational remainders through explicit Synthesis Refusal labeling.

**Susanne Langer (1895–1985)** distinguishes between discursive symbolism (language and logic) and presentational symbolism (art, music, and the direct articulation of feeling and lived experience). This distinction supports the framework’s capacity to treat different modes of expression—linguistic, phenomenal, and artistic—without forcing all pointing into propositional form, while preserving the living tension between what can be relationally constructed and what remains as non-relational remainder.

**Ernst Cassirer (1874–1945)**, in his Philosophy of Symbolic Forms, treats symbolic formation (myth, language, science, and art) as fundamental ways in which reality is expressed and constituted. His account of symbolic expression as an active, world-constituting process offers a broad philosophical parallel to the framework’s treatment of expression as its fundamental primitive and to the spectrum of relational modes through which pointing occurs.

## **P4 – Layering in Expressions**

P4 registers that relateds and relators organize into hierarchical expressions—subatomic (singles), atomic (sequences), and complex (nests)—while remaining open to perceptual role shifts under P8.

**Aristotle (384–322 BCE)** develops hylomorphism, in which things are composites of matter (hylē) shaped by form (morphē). This offers a classical prototype for P4’s layered expressions without committing the framework to substance metaphysics.

**Plotinus (204–270 CE)** describes reality through emanation in hierarchical levels from the One. This provides a provisional model for P4’s layering spectrum while the framework rejects any fixed ontological priority or teleological necessity.

**Alfred North Whitehead (again)** supplies in his account of societies and nexuses of actual occasions a processual, non-substantial basis for hierarchical layering that remains fully compatible with P8 perceptual renewal and Functional Role Shift.

## **P5 – Plurality of Expressions**

P5 registers that layered expressions manifest as a current primary expression co-existing with provisional secondary expressions on a spectrum of multiplicity (solitary, finite, or infinite).

**William James (1842–1910) and John Dewey (1859–1952)** develop classical American pragmatism, which treats truth and meaning as clarified by lived consequences across multiple contexts. This grounds P5’s co-existence of primary and secondary expressions and the toolkit’s contextual calibration without appeal to absolute substrates.

**Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz (1646–1716)** articulates a monadology of innumerable simple substances, each reflecting the whole from its own perspective. This illustrates P5’s plurality without reducing to solipsism or requiring a single privileged viewpoint.

**Willard Van Orman Quine (1908–2000)** advances ontological relativity and the indeterminacy of translation, demonstrating that what exists is always relative to a chosen conceptual scheme. This supports P5’s provisional secondary expressions and the framework’s refusal of any absolute substrate.

## **P6 – Comparability and Measurement of Expressions**

P6 registers that plurality enables a spectrum of alignability and transduction, producing distances and divergences that can be measured as utilities.

**Rudolf Carnap (1891–1970)** develops logical empiricism and verificationist criteria that inform the dense/symmetric versus partial/asymmetric alignability spectrum, while the framework broadens these criteria equitably to include phenomenal and transduced inputs.

Information-theoretic divergences, particularly the Jensen-Shannon divergence (symmetric) and Kullback-Leibler divergence (asymmetric), directly shape the toolkit’s resonance, stability, and alignment metrics. These enable precise quantification of relational connectivity without reducing meaning to pure information.

## **P7 – Evaluation and Yield**

P7 aggregates certainties and uncertainties into provisional dialectical yields (balanced, tensive, tempered, or raw) without requiring a separate “chain holds” gate. The yield spectrum itself encodes self-reflexive evaluation.

**G.W.F. Hegel (1770–1831)** supplies the dialectical spirit of progress through negation and sublation. The framework adopts this spirit for P7’s certainty–uncertainty aggregation while stripping it of teleological necessity and any claim to final synthesis.

**Charles Sanders Peirce (again)** combines abduction (inference to the best explanation) with permanent fallibilism. These underwrite P7’s presumption-tempered yields and the toolkit’s Phase 5 reflexive certainty tempering.

**Karl Popper (1902–1994)** advances falsificationism and critical rationalism. These directly shape P7’s yield adjudication, the toolkit’s binomial incoherence thresholds (p \= 0.18 with \+5% reflexive tolerance), and the commitment to testable provisionality confirmed in the metric validation results.

## **P8 – Perception/Interpretation and the Renewal Loop**

P8 registers that once a yield is reached, perception determines whether it stands as provisionally complete (Door 1\) or opens to any form of re-expression, refinement, modular incorporation, or outright discard (Door 2). It functions as the living renewal mechanism of the schema.

**Nāgārjuna’s Madhyamaka (again)** achieves a middle way through negation of extremes without final closure. This parallels P8’s open perception gate and perpetual renewal loop.

**Pyrrho of Elis (c. 360–270 BCE)** models epochē (suspension of judgment) as a mindful pause that keeps the P8 gate genuinely open rather than prematurely closed.

**Heinz von Foerster (1911–2002)** develops second-order cybernetics, in which systems observe themselves observing. This directly informs P8’s reflexive renewal and the toolkit’s Phase 5 certainty tempering, in which the system applies its own grammar to its own outputs.

**Hans-Georg Gadamer (1900–2002)** articulates the event of understanding as a “fusion of horizons,” in which the interpreter’s horizon and the horizon of the text or phenomenon are brought into productive tension and transformed. This offers a powerful parallel to P8’s perception gate, where re-expression and renewal arise through genuine encounter rather than through the application of fixed criteria, and where the act of understanding itself modifies the pointing under examination.

## **Formal, Mathematical, and Computational Influences**

The toolkit and unified mathematical model operationalize the P1–P8 distinctions through continuous spectra, hybrid functions, normalized Bayesian procedures, dilemma gates, tetralemma and pentalemma mappings, and derived metrics including the Secondary Coherence Score and Inferred Relator Mode Distribution. These technical resources draw upon several formal traditions.

**Lotfi Zadeh (1921–2017)** introduces fuzzy set theory and fuzzy membership functions. These supply the conceptual foundation for the hybrid function H(x) \= min(1, μ(x) × P\_norm(x | V)) and the linear and clamped sigmoid membership functions employed across the presumptions and toolkit phases. Fuzzy approaches enable graded, non-binary distinctions that align with the framework’s commitment to continuous spectra rather than sharp ontological cuts.

Bayesian epistemology and normalized posterior procedures provide the mechanism for updating under uncertainty. The normalized Bayesian posterior supports the abduction of secondary expressions in Phase 2 and the computation of alignment and traction metrics while remaining compatible with the framework’s fallibilism and equity mechanisms.

**Claude Shannon (1916–2001)** establishes information theory and entropy as measures of uncertainty and surprise. Shannon entropy provides the quantitative foundation for the toolkit’s resonance, stability, uncertainty resonance, and uncertainty stability metrics. The framework employs these measures diagnostically while refusing to reduce the living tension of meaning to information alone.

**Kiyoshi Itō (1915–2008) and Gisiro Maruyama** develop stochastic differential equations and the Euler-Maruyama method. These enable the toolkit’s modeling of porous flows, sigma widening for phenomenal inputs, and chaos-scale equity adjustments without forcing high-variance or non-linguistic inputs into rigid relational categories.

The cybernetic tradition, initiated by **Norbert Wiener** and extended into second-order cybernetics by **Heinz von Foerster**, supplies conceptual resources for reflexive, circular, and self-observing systems. Von Foerster’s work on the observer-inclusive nature of knowing directly informs P8’s reflexive renewal loop and Phase 5 certainty tempering.

**Niklas Luhmann (1927–1998)** develops a theory of autopoietic social systems grounded in second-order observation. His account of systems that observe their own observing operations provides a powerful parallel to P8’s renewal loop and to the toolkit’s capacity to apply its own grammar reflexively to its outputs. Luhmann’s distinction between system and environment also resonates with the framework’s operational separation between relational construction and protected non-relational remainders.

**Gilbert Simondon (1924–1989)** articulates a philosophy of individuation centered on transduction—the relational operation by which structure and organization propagate from one domain or element to another. His realism of relations, in which relations are constitutive rather than secondary to pre-existing substances, offers a powerful conceptual parallel to P3’s relational construction and P6’s spectrum of transduction and alignability.

**Francisco Varela (1946–2001)**, working with **Humberto Maturana**, develops the concepts of autopoiesis and structural coupling. Autopoiesis models the self-referential, operationally closed yet informationally open character of living systems, directly paralleling the framework’s treatment of secondary synthesis as the engine through which primary expressions obtain. Structural coupling describes the relational engagement through which systems and their environments co-emerge, offering a strong biological and cognitive parallel to P3’s relational construction and to the toolkit’s emphasis on pointing as enacted relation.

The digital physics and computational ontology tradition, including **Konrad Zuse**, **Edward Fredkin**, **Stephen Wolfram**, and **Seth Lloyd**, along with **Melvin Vopson**’s Second Law of Infodynamics (2022–2026), supplies empirical signatures tested in live toolkit runs on questions such as the Simulation Hypothesis. These converging lines function as strong but non-deductive evidence. When computational or energetic limits exceed current relational traction, the framework registers them explicitly as Synthesis Refusal.

**Kurt Gödel**’s incompleteness theorems demonstrate inherent limits to formal systems. This supplies a powerful modern parallel to the non-relational remainder and Synthesis Refusal: certain truths or structures necessarily exceed what any given formal system can capture from within. The framework registers such limits operationally rather than as positive ontological claims.

**Alfred Tarski**’s semantic theory of truth and work on semantic closure address the conditions under which truth predicates can be consistently defined. These insights inform the framework’s treatment of repeatability, the distinction between object-level and meta-level expressions, and the deliberate avoidance of self-referential paradoxes that would collapse the distinction between relational construction and non-relational gaps.

These formal resources do not constitute foundational axioms. They function as operational tools whose outputs remain subject to P7 dialectical adjudication and P8 renewal. The metric validation results (May 2026\) confirm that the integrated system of weights, clamps, and heuristics behaves stably, directionally correctly, and equitably across tested ranges and boundary cases.
